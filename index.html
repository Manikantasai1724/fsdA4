The Problem

Why Scheduling Is Broken in Most Colleges

Manual timetabling leads to cascading failures — from double-booked rooms to burned-out faculty and frustrated students.

🚫

Classroom Double-Booking

Two classes scheduled in the same room at the same time — a common disaster in manual systems that disrupts entire departments.

⏰

Faculty Overload & Conflicts

Professors assigned to multiple classes simultaneously, leading to absenteeism, poor teaching quality, and staff burnout.

🔄

Last-Minute Changes

One schedule modification cascades into dozens of changes, consuming hours of admin time with every revision cycle.

📊

No Real-Time Visibility

Stakeholders operate on outdated spreadsheets with no live view of room availability, faculty load, or schedule conflicts.

🎓

Student Experience Suffers

Poorly scheduled timetables leave students with back-to-back classes, long gaps, or clashing exam slots with no recourse.

🏗

Underutilized Infrastructure

Labs and large lecture halls remain empty while smaller rooms are overcrowded — pure resource waste with no optimization.
